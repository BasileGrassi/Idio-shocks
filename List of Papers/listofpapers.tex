\section{Policy distortions and aggregate productivity with heterogenous establishments, Restuccia and Rogerson 2008}
\nocite{Rest08}

Ideas-repec link \url{http://ideas.repec.org/a/red/issued/07-48.html}
\subsection{Main message}
In a general equilibirum model \emph{à la} Hopenhayn (1992)\nocite{Hope92}, authors studie the reallocation effect of a large range of policy. They calibrated the undistorded economy to the US. They study the effect of taxe on the output price correlated and uncorrelated to the productivity level. They show that when the taxe are not correlated the effect of a 40\% taxe leads to a drop in TFP (only TFP, they correct for the effect on capital accumulation) of 8\%. In the case of subsiding low productivity firms and taxing high productivity firms, the same taxe eads to a drop of TFP of 31\% only due to misallocation of output(?).
